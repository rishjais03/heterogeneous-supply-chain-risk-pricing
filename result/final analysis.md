# Key Findings

## 1. NSCR Effect

The Network Supply Chain Risk (NSCR) coefficient is positive, suggesting that firms with greater supply chain exposure may experience higher expected returns. However, the result is not statistically significant at conventional significance levels.

This indicates:

* weak empirical evidence for heterogeneous pricing of supply chain risk,
* but the positive coefficient direction remains economically meaningful.

---

## 2. Market Factors

The Fama–French market factor (Mkt-RF) is highly significant, confirming that broader market conditions strongly influence firm-level excess returns.

---

## 3. Size Effect

The SMB factor is statistically significant and positive, indicating that smaller firms tended to generate higher excess returns during the sample period.

---

## 4. Value Effect

The HML coefficient is negative but statistically insignificant, suggesting weak evidence for a value premium effect within the sample.

---

# Overall Conclusion

The empirical results provide partial support for the hypothesis that supply chain risk influences expected returns across firms embedded within production networks.

While the NSCR variable exhibits the expected positive relationship with returns, statistical significance remains limited after controlling for standard Fama–French risk factors.

The findings suggest that:

* production network exposure may influence financial risk transmission,
* but the pricing of supply chain risk is likely heterogeneous and dependent on firm-specific characteristics and market conditions.
