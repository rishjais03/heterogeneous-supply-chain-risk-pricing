# Heterogeneous Pricing of Supply Chain Risk in Production Networks

## Overview

This repository contains the empirical analysis, econometric modeling, and supporting materials for the research project:

**“Heterogeneous Pricing of Supply Chain Risk in Production Networks”**

The study investigates whether supply chain risk is heterogeneously priced across firms embedded within production networks. Using firm-level financial data, production network structures, macroeconomic shock exposure, and empirical asset pricing methodologies, the analysis evaluates how supply chain disruptions affect expected stock returns.

---

## Research Motivation

Modern firms operate within highly interconnected production networks where shocks can propagate through supplier-customer relationships. Traditional financial risk models often overlook these network-based dependencies.

This project introduces a framework to measure Network Supply Chain Risk (NSCR) and examines whether firms with greater exposure to supply chain disruptions exhibit different expected return dynamics.

---

## Research Contribution

This study contributes to the literature on:

- Empirical Asset Pricing
- Production Networks
- Financial Econometrics
- Supply Chain Risk
- Quantitative Finance

The paper constructs a firm-level **Network Supply Chain Risk (NSCR)** measure using weighted supply chain exposure and macroeconomic shock transmission mechanisms.

The empirical analysis provides evidence that:

- Supply chain risk may influence expected returns
- Network exposure affects financial risk transmission
- Risk pricing varies heterogeneously across firms

## Methodology

The analysis includes:

- Panel-data econometric modeling
- Fama–French factor integration
- Network-based exposure estimation
- Weighted shock propagation analysis
- Fixed-effects regression models
- Robustness testing procedures
